# Product Vision

## Vision Statement

**Novelle** will become the world's most intelligent, personalized, and community-driven reading platform — the place where every reader discovers their next great book, deepens their relationship with reading, and connects with a global community of people who love books as much as they do.

---

## Why Novelle Exists

Novelle exists because the reading world deserves better than what it has today.

Goodreads — the dominant platform with 150M+ users — has been effectively abandoned by Amazon. Its UI is frozen in 2007. Its recommendation engine is broken. There are no half-star ratings, no DNF shelf, no meaningful stats, no dark mode, no AI features, and no real community safety tools. Users stay because of inertia and data lock-in, not because the product serves them well.

Alternatives like StoryGraph, Hardcover, Literal, and Fable each improve on specific pieces but none deliver the complete picture:

- **StoryGraph** has great stats and mood tags but no AI, weak book clubs, and slow development
- **Hardcover** is beautiful but skews heavily toward sci-fi/fantasy and has performance issues
- **Literal** is clean and free but limited in scope
- **Fable** focuses on celebrity-led clubs rather than user-driven discovery

Novelle will fill this gap by combining **best-in-class reading analytics**, **AI-powered reading companionship**, **rich social features**, and **genuinely useful discovery** into a single platform — without being owned by a retailer, without ads as the business model, and without treating readers as products.

---

## The Problem

Readers today face five core problems that no single platform solves:

1. **Discovery is broken** — Recommendations are generic, uninspired, and don't understand what you actually feel like reading
2. **Tracking is shallow** — Star ratings and "read/reading/want-to-read" are not enough to capture a reading life
3. **Community is toxic or absent** — Goodreads has review-bombing and harassment; alternatives lack critical mass
4. **Data is locked in** — Switching platforms means losing years of reading history
5. **No intelligent layer** — No platform helps you *while you read* — discussing, explaining, quizzing, or coaching
