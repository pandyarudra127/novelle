# Problem Definition

## Why Does Novelle Exist?

Novelle exists because the book tracking and discovery market is broken. The dominant player (Goodreads) has been neglected for over a decade by its owner (Amazon). Newer alternatives improve on specific aspects but fail to deliver a complete, modern reading experience. Readers deserve a platform that combines the best of everything — tracking, analytics, AI, community, and discovery — without being owned by a retailer, without selling user data, and without treating the reader as a product.

---

## What Problems Does Goodreads Have?

1. **No meaningful investment** — Amazon acquired Goodreads in 2013 and has barely updated it since. The UI is outdated, features are missing, and bugs go unfixed for years.
2. **Broken recommendations** — The recommendation engine is famously bad, offering generic suggestions that ignore user reading history.
3. **Missing basic features** — No half-star ratings, no DNF shelf, no dark mode, no content warnings, no proper book club tools, no analytics.
4. **Review bombing & harassment** — Authors and readers face targeted harassment with no meaningful moderation or guardrails.
5. **No customer support** — Users report generic, unhelpful responses or no response at all.
6. **App is buggy** — Frequent crashes, login issues, sync problems, error messages.
7. **Data lock-in** — While export is technically possible, the friction of switching keeps users locked in despite dissatisfaction.
8. **Amazon ownership** — Every interaction serves Amazon's commercial priorities. Privacy and independence are compromised.

## Why Would Users Switch?

- **Better features** — Half-star ratings, DNF shelf, mood-based discovery, AI companion, analytics
- **Modern design** — Clean UI, dark mode, responsive, fast
- **AI augmentation** — Reading companion, spoiler-free Q&A, discussion questions, quizzes
- **Real book clubs** — Not threads but actual club tools (chat, polls, voting, scheduling)
- **Data ownership** — Import from Goodreads, export anytime, no lock-in
- **Privacy & independence** — No ads, no data selling, not owned by a retailer
- **Active development** — A platform that actually improves, listens to users, and ships features

## Who Is the Target Audience?

### Primary
- **Avid fiction readers** (20-40 books/year) who want better tracking and discovery
- **Book club members and organizers** who need coordination tools
- **Non-fiction/personal development readers** who want notes, highlights, and AI summaries
- **Genre readers** (sci-fi, fantasy, romance, thriller) who want mood-based discovery
- **Readers switching from Goodreads** who are actively looking for an alternative

### Secondary
- Students who need reading tracking and comprehension tools
- Audiobook listeners who want format-specific tracking
- Casual readers who want a beautiful, simple way to track their reading
- Authors and publishers who want verified profiles and discovery tools
