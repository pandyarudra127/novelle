# User Personas

---

## Persona 1: Emma Chen — The Casual Fiction Reader

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Age** | 26 |
| **Occupation** | Marketing Manager |
| **Location** | Austin, TX |
| **Devices** | iPhone 15, iPad, Kindle Paperwhite |
| **Favorite Genres** | Literary fiction, contemporary romance, thrillers |
| **Books Per Year** | 20-30 |
| **Tech Comfort** | High |

### Reading Habits
Emma reads 2-3 books per month, mostly on her Kindle during commutes and before bed. She uses Goodreads because everyone she knows uses it, but she's frustrated by how ugly it is and how bad the recommendations are. She'd love to track her reading better but doesn't want to spend time manually updating things.

### Goals
- Find her next read without endless scrolling
- See what her friends are reading and enjoying
- Get better recommendations that understand her taste
- Track her reading in a way that feels satisfying, not like homework
- Share her reading life on social media with beautiful stats

### Pain Points
- Goodreads recommendations are generic and useless ("If you liked The Great Gatsby, try...")
- No way to know if she'll like the pacing or tone of a book before starting
- Half the time she tries to log a book on Goodreads, the app crashes
- Can't get a quick, spoiler-free sense of whether a book is for her
- Has no idea what her reading patterns actually look like

### Why Novelle?
"I want an app that feels as good as the books I read. I want to open it and feel inspired, not like I'm using a database from 2005."

## Persona 2: Marcus Johnson — The Avid Reader & Book Club Host

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Age** | 34 |
| **Occupation** | High School English Teacher |
| **Location** | Portland, OR |
| **Devices** | Pixel 8, MacBook Air, Kobo Libra |
| **Favorite Genres** | Sci-fi, fantasy, literary fiction, graphic novels |
| **Books Per Year** | 60-80 |
| **Tech Comfort** | Medium-High |

### Reading Habits
Marcus reads voraciously across genres. He runs two book clubs — one with friends and one with students. He's always on the lookout for discussion questions, thematic analysis, and ways to make reading more social. He has used Goodreads for over a decade and has 1,200+ books logged, but he's fed up with the lack of progress.

### Goals
- Run engaging book club meetings with minimal prep
- Discover books that his clubs would enjoy discussing
- Track his massive TBR pile and actually make progress on it
- Analyze his reading patterns over time (genres, authors, formats)
- Get AI-generated discussion questions and thematic analysis

### Pain Points
- Book club coordination is a nightmare — WhatsApp + email + Goodreads = chaos
- Coming up with discussion questions every month is exhausting
- His TBR list is 400+ books and completely unmanageable
- Goodreads gives him zero useful stats about his reading
- No way to see how his reading tastes have evolved over time

### Why Novelle?
"I need a command center for my reading life. One place where I can track my books, run my clubs, and actually understand my reading habits — without managing five different apps."

## Persona 3: Priya Patel — The Mindful Reader & Self-Improvement Seeker

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Age** | 29 |
| **Occupation** | Product Designer |
| **Location** | San Francisco, CA |
| **Devices** | iPhone 15, iPad Pro, Oasis (rarely used) |
| **Favorite Genres** | Self-help, psychology, memoirs, business, productivity |
| **Books Per Year** | 40-50 |
| **Tech Comfort** | Very High |

### Reading Habits
Priya reads mostly non-fiction and listens to audiobooks during her commute. She takes extensive notes, highlights passages constantly, and sets ambitious reading goals. She loves data and wants detailed analytics about her reading. She's tried StoryGraph, Fable, and Literal but finds each one lacking something critical. She wants a reading coach, not just a tracker.

### Goals
- Get AI-powered insights and summaries of the books she reads
- Set and track ambitious reading goals with granular progress data
- Take notes and highlights that she can actually use later
- Get mood-based recommendations that understand her context
- See beautiful, shareable analytics of her reading life

### Pain Points
- StoryGraph has great stats but no AI — she has to use ChatGPT separately for summaries and insights
- None of the platforms help her retain or apply what she reads
- She wants a reading coach that adapts to her pace and comprehension
- Highlighting on Kindle is great but extracting and organizing those highlights is painful
- She reads across formats (ebook, audio, physical) and no platform tracks all of them well

### Why Novelle?
"I want an AI reading companion that knows what I'm reading, where I am in the book, and can discuss it with me. I want to finish a chapter and ask 'what was the key insight here?' without spoiling myself. No app does this today."
